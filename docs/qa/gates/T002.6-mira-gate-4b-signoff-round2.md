### §3.4 IC=0.866 — engineering correctness vs strategy edge interpretation

The Round 2 question raised by the user task hint: is IC=0.866 **real edge** masked by costs (DSR=0.767), or **measurement artifact / leakage** in IC pairing?

**Mira authoritative call: real prediction-level discriminator power, NOT leakage.**

Reasoning:

1. **Predictor-label timestamp ordering:** predictor `predictor_signal_per_trade` is captured at entry-decision time (`entry_fill.ts ∈ {16:55, 17:10, 17:25, 17:40} BRT` per spec §2.2); label `forward_return_at_1755_pts` is the close-to-close return from `entry_fill.price` to session-tape's 17:55:00 BRT close (or last trade ≤ 17:55:00 BRT per Nova spec §6 yaml block `auction.exit_price_rule`) per spec §15.3. Predictor uses ONLY data ≤ entry timestamp; label is the realization in [entry_ts, 17:55:00] window. Forward-window 15-60 minutes; **no temporal leakage** (entry_ts < 17:55 strictly; predictor inputs `intraday_flow_direction` are accumulated up to entry_ts only).

2. **Predictor distribution:** `-intraday_flow_direction` at entry under `filter_active(event)` (`|intraday_flow_magnitude| > P_lower_or_upper AND ATR_dia_ratio ∈ [P20, P80]`) is **bimodal** (mostly +1 for short-fade trials and -1 for long-fade trials, with magnitude variation from `intraday_flow_magnitude`). Spearman rank correlation between a strongly bimodal predictor and a continuous label can saturate near ±1 if the predictor consistently ranks-correlates with label sign. The H1 alternative thesis predicts EXACTLY this — end-of-day inventory unwind in WDO produces a fade reversion to 17:55 close, so when predictor (negative of intraday flow) is positive, expected forward return is positive. The 0.866 magnitude reflects strong systematic regularity under the inventory-unwind hypothesis.

3. **AFML §8.6 IC stability calibration:** "IC > 0.05 is meaningful; IC > 0.5 is exceptional". 0.866 is far above exceptional. Caveat: AFML §8.6 is calibrated for cross-sectional asset returns at daily frequency — not intraday WDO end-of-day fade events — so direct comparability is loose. The exceptional reading should be checked against the strategy's structural predictability; a known structural pattern (custodian flows / inventory unwind at close) can produce IC orders of magnitude higher than typical cross-sectional fund-management IC ranges.

4. **Hit rate paradox (the diagnostic key):** `hit_rate = 0.4966981` (essentially 50/50 PnL win-rate at the trade level) coexists with IC = 0.866. This is the **canonical cost-and-friction signature**: rank correlation between predictor and forward-return label is strong (event-level edge), but realized PnL is approximately break-even (after costs + slippage + triple-barrier early exits). If IC=0.866 were a leakage artifact (predictor encoding label information), hit_rate would track IC tightly (0.95+ range). Hit_rate = 0.497 with IC = 0.866 is **inconsistent with leakage** and consistent with **cost-eroded edge**: the prediction is correct in rank-order, but the magnitude of forward return is not large enough to overcome triple-barrier early exits + cost atlas + latency_dma2 slippage at the hit-rate level. This is precisely the `costed_out_edge` sub-classification.

5. **Spec §15.7 dedup invariant satisfied:** per-event keys `(session_date, trial_id, entry_window_brt)` first-occurrence dedup ensures 3800 events are unique — no event-overcounting that could artificially inflate IC by treating overlapping CPCV folds as independent samples.

**Verdict on IC interpretation:** IC=0.866 is **engineering-correct measurement of real prediction-level discriminator power** — rank-correlated with forward-return label as the H1 alternative thesis predicts. It is **NOT a leakage artifact**. It is **NOT measurement noise**. It is **NOT engineering signal only** (i.e., not just the wiring-fixed analog of Round 1's IC=0).
