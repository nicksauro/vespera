## §1 RLP (Retail Liquidity Provider) policy — WDO

### §1.1 Definition (B3 microstructure)

RLP is a B3-credentialed program where a broker's own market-maker desk matches retail order-flow against the broker's internal book **before** the order reaches the public matching engine (PUMA). When matched, the trade prints as `tradeType=13` on the consolidated tape (Nelo `TNewTradeCallback` decode reference; cost atlas `costs.brokerage.notes` line 167).

The RLP fill is **not** a price-discovery event in the traditional book-driven sense — it does not move best_bid / best_ask. It is liquidity sintética cruzada com varejo, regulamentada por Ofício Circular B3 RCP-*/2019.

### §1.2 Active hours WDO

[WEB-CONFIRMED 2026-04-22 — Rico, XP retail program documentation, cross-referenced in cost atlas sources `RICO-CORRETAGEM`, `XP-CORRETAGEM`]:

- **RLP active:** continuous trading window, **09:00 — 17:55 BRT** (i.e., the entire continuous-session phase).
- **RLP inactive:**
  - Pre-opening (08:55 — 09:00 BRT) — leilão de determinação; no continuous matching.
  - Opening auction (09:00 — 09:30 BRT) — leilão de abertura; trades print `tradeType=4`, not RLP.
  - Closing call (17:55 — 18:00 BRT) — closing auction; print is `tradeType=4`.
  - After 18:00 BRT — market closed; WDO has no after-market.

**Implication for T002.6 strategy windows (entry 16:55 / 17:10 / 17:25 / 17:40, exit 17:55):** all four entry windows fall **inside** RLP active hours; exit at 17:55 BRT sits **at the boundary** between RLP-active and closing-call (see §3 Auction states).

### §1.3 Detection in trade tape

**Live regime (Nelo TNewTradeCallback):** `tradeType=13` is the canonical detection signal. Field name in our schema: `trade_type` (Nelo Q07-V enum decode).

**Historic regime (our parquet dataset `D:\sentinel_data\historical\`):** **RLP IS NOT IDENTIFIABLE** at the `trade_type` enum level — parquet `aggressor` column only carries `BUY` / `SELL` / `NONE` (see Nova features-availability matrix `historic_gaps_in_our_parquet`). RLP fills appear inside the `BUY`/`SELL` aggressor stream without a discriminator flag.

This is a known dataset gap. Phase F (T002.6) operates on the historic parquet, therefore **RLP-segregated CVD / volume-profile features are not computable historically**. Live-mode Phase H will recover the discriminator.

### §1.4 Phase F implication — fill treatment recommendation

Three fill-treatment options analyzed:

| Option | Treatment | Rationale | Verdict |
|--------|-----------|-----------|---------|
| A | Instant fill at decision price; zero slippage; zero latency | Models pure RLP "instant match" — only correct **if** the order is in fact RLP-matched, which is a per-trade unknown (the broker decides) | **REJECTED** — overestimates execution quality; assumes 100% RLP match-rate which is empirically false (RLP share fluctuates 10-30% intraday per cost atlas line 167-168) |
| B | Standard slippage + `latency_dma2_profile` latency on **all fills**; treat RLP as one of the slippage realizations baked into the latency model | Conservative; consistent with cost atlas brokerage default 0.00 (assumes RLP is operating, but does not skip slippage) | **RECOMMENDED — Option B** |
| C | Mixed: 20% of fills instant (proxy RLP-matched share), 80% standard slippage | Attempts realism but introduces an unverifiable parameter (the 20% mix) into an already-thin spec; backtest result becomes sensitive to a stochastic knob with no empirical anchor in our dataset | **REJECTED** — invents a feature absent from data (Article IV violation for Phase F) |

**Nova recommendation: Option B.** Slippage is uniformly applied via `latency_dma2_profile`; cost atlas brokerage `per_contract_one_way: 0.00` already reflects the RLP-active retail fee regime. The strategy is not penalized for RLP being on (zero brokerage), but is also not given a fictitious instant-fill bonus. RLP becomes a future live-mode optimization (T002.7+ Phase G/H), not a Phase F edge component.

### §1.5 Edge cases (R-5)

- **Cross trades (`tradeType=1`):** broker-internal negotiated trade; **NOT** price discovery; **NOT** RLP. Flag separately (see §5). Phase F: exclude from CVD; do NOT treat as a tradeable signal.
- **Leilão (`tradeType=4`):** results from open/close auction — not continuous matching, not RLP-eligible. See §3.
- **Circuit breakers:** B3 halt (10/15/20% triggers) suspends continuous trading; RLP suspends with it. See §4.
- **Rollover D-3..D-1:** RLP continues to operate on the front-month contract but volume migrates to back-month — RLP fill-rate may degrade on D-1 as front-month liquidity thins. See §2.

## §2 Rollover D-3..D-1 transitions

### §2.1 Definition (B3 mechanics)

WDO is a monthly contract (codes F/G/H/J/K/M/N/Q/U/V/X/Z). Vencimento = **1º dia útil do mês de entrega** per `config/calendar/2024-2027.yaml` line 109 ("Regra B3: vencimento = 1º dia útil do mês"). The `wdo_expirations` block lists 36 expiry dates (2024-2026).

Rollover **D-3..D-1** = the three trading sessions immediately preceding `wdo_expirations[i]`. During this window, open interest and volume migrate from the expiring front-month contract to the next-month contract. The synthetic ticker WDOFUT re-points to the new front-month at the rollover transition.

### §2.2 Volume signature D-3..D-1

Empirical pattern (Nova observation, validated against B3 historical OI data, [TO-VERIFY for our specific dataset window — Beckett Phase F empirical confirm]):

- **D-3:** front-month still dominant (~70-80% volume share); back-month begins climbing. Roll-spread trading (calendar spread) becomes visible.
- **D-2:** crossover point; front and back roughly balanced (~50/50). Bid-ask spread on front-month widens as market makers withdraw inventory.
- **D-1:** back-month dominant (~70-80%); front-month liquidity thins materially. Last trading session for the expiring contract.
- **D+0 (expiry day):** front-month delivers / settles; new front-month is the contract that was "back" yesterday; WDOFUT alias points to the new front from session start.

### §2.3 Calendar consumption

`config/calendar/2024-2027.yaml` exposes `wdo_expirations: [list of dates]`. The Phase F harness **MUST** derive D-3, D-2, D-1 by walking back **3 trading days** (skipping `br_holidays`) from each expiry date. Naive calendar-day arithmetic is **incorrect** because BR holidays interject.

Pseudocode (canonical):
```
def rollover_window(expiry: date, br_holidays: set[date]) -> set[date]:
    out = set()
    d = expiry
    found = 0
    while found < 3:
        d = d - timedelta(days=1)
        if d.weekday() < 5 and d not in br_holidays:
            out.add(d)
            found += 1
    return out  # {D-3, D-2, D-1}
```

Test fixture for AC4 (`tests/cpcv_harness/test_rollover_transitions.py`): assert `rollover_window(2026-04-01, br_holidays_2026) == {2026-03-27, 2026-03-30, 2026-03-31}` (since 2026-04-01 is the next April expiry; the three preceding trading days are 27/Mar Friday, 30/Mar Monday, 31/Mar Tuesday — no holiday interjects).

### §2.4 Strategy logic implication — option analysis

Three options analyzed:

| Option | Treatment | Sample-size impact | Edge-attribution risk | Verdict |
|--------|-----------|--------------------|-----------------------|---------|
| A — skip D-3..D-1 sessions entirely | Defensive; protects strategy from artificial price action driven by roll-spread trading | -3 sessions/month × 36 months = **-108 sessions** lost from Phase F sample | Low (strategy clean from rollover noise) | REJECTED — sample-size cost too high; AC9 floor (≥150-250 events) at risk |
| **B — flag D-3..D-1 as `rollover_window=True` per session row; allow trading; preserve in sample for attribution analysis post-N7** | Sample-size preserved; clean attribution lane established | Zero loss | Mitigated via flag (Riven 3-bucket can isolate `rollover_window` rows in attribution) | **RECOMMENDED — Option B** |
| C — full inclusion without distinction | Sample-size preserved | High — cannot post-hoc isolate rollover-noise contribution to N7 metrics; if DSR bumps from rollover artifacts, we cannot attribute | REJECTED per Nova authority — violates ESC-011 R9 3-bucket attribution discipline (`engineering_wiring` vs `strategy_edge` vs `inconclusive` requires per-row provenance) |

**Nova recommendation: Option B.** Phase F harness emits per-session feature `rollover_window: bool` (True iff session-date ∈ D-3..D-1 set for any expiry in calendar). The flag is consumed downstream by:
1. Riven post-mortem 3-bucket attribution (`docs/risk/post-mortems/T002-synthetic-vs-real-tape-attribution.md`) — separate lane to inspect rollover-rows DSR contribution.
2. Mira Gate 4b clearance — if DSR>0.95 holds **only** because rollover rows inflate the metric, that is an `engineering_wiring` artifact, not `strategy_edge`. Flag enables the diagnostic.

### §2.5 Front-month carry-forward bug (R-6)

**Critical bug-class to prevent:** at D+0 the WDOFUT synthetic alias re-points to the new front-month. If the harness loads the parquet for date D+0 by ticker `WDOFUT` (or by the previous front-month code), it may end up reading the **back-month contract's** trade-tape under the **front-month's** label, producing an artificial price discontinuity.

Mitigation (mandatory in Dex T1 impl per AC4):
1. The harness MUST resolve the explicit contract code (e.g., `WDOJ26`) for each session-date by intersecting against `wdo_expirations`.
2. Trade-tape concatenation across the rollover boundary MUST flag the boundary explicitly; no naive `pd.concat` of front+back without a session-boundary marker.
3. Test fixture asserts: `session = D+0`, contract resolved = back-month code, no carry-forward of front-month last-trade price into D+0 first-trade calculation.

## §3 Auction states (open / close)

### §3.1 Open auction (08:55 — 09:30 BRT)

- **08:55 — 09:00:** pre-opening (leilão de determinação) — orders accepted, no continuous matching.
- **09:00 — 09:30:** opening auction proper — at 09:00 the auction fires (or shortly thereafter following desbalanceio); a burst of `tradeType=4` prints in <1 second; book then transitions to continuous mode.

**Phase F implication:** T002.6 entry windows (16:55 / 17:10 / 17:25 / 17:40 BRT) are all **late-day**, well clear of open auction. The thesis (`T002-end-of-day-inventory-unwind-wdo`) is structurally end-of-day. **Open auction has zero direct impact on T002.6 strategy logic.**

However, sessions where the opening auction fired late (post-09:30 due to large desbalanceio) may signal extreme intraday regime — Nova flags as future feature for T002.7+, not Phase F.

### §3.2 Close auction (17:55 — 18:00 BRT)

- **17:55:** continuous matching ceases; closing call (leilão de fechamento) begins.
- **17:55 — 18:00:** orders accepted into the call; matching engine determines the closing print (preço de ajuste) at 18:00.
- **18:00:** closing print fires — `tradeType=4`. This is the **ajuste diário** used for margin computation.

**Phase F implication — CRITICAL:** T002.6 triple-barrier exit at **17:55 BRT** sits **AT** the close-auction boundary. Three handling sub-options:

| Sub-option | Exit treatment | Rationale | Verdict |
|------------|---------------|-----------|---------|
| 3.2-α | Exit at last continuous trade strictly **before** 17:55:00 BRT (e.g., 17:54:59.x) | Avoids contamination from auction print; uses last-known continuous price; consistent with continuous-matching slippage model in `latency_dma2_profile` | **RECOMMENDED — Nova authority** |
| 3.2-β | Exit at the 18:00 closing print (`tradeType=4`) | Uses the ajuste; conceptually clean for "end-of-session" exit but defers exit by 5 minutes vs spec | REJECTED — alters T002 thesis exit semantics; spec yaml v0.2.3 says 17:55 |
| 3.2-γ | Exit at 17:55:00 BRT exactly at whatever print is closest | Ambiguous; if 17:55:00 falls inside the call no continuous price exists | REJECTED — undefined behavior at boundary |

**Nova recommendation: 3.2-α.** Mira spec T0a MUST encode this in the exit logic: "exit price = last `tradeType ∈ {1,2,3,13}` print with timestamp < 17:55:00 BRT on session date". This excludes auction prints (`tradeType=4`) from forming the exit price.

Configurable knob exposed: `auction.exclude_open_close_minutes: 5` (default 5; means the last 5 minutes of the session — 17:55 to 18:00 — are excluded from continuous-price extraction). Squad may tune this in v1.1.0 if calibration data motivates.

## §4 Circuit breakers + market halts

### §4.1 B3 thresholds

B3 derivative circuit-breaker thresholds applicable to WDO:
- **-10% intraday move:** trading halt 30 minutes.
- **-15% intraday move:** additional 60-minute halt.
- **-20% intraday move:** session terminates early.

(Reference: B3 Regulamento de Operações; Nova session-phases atlas. Exact symmetric thresholds for upside moves are similar in regime but historically rare for WDO.)

### §4.2 Phase F implication

Sessions with circuit-breaker firings exhibit **discontinuous price formation** during the halt and **abnormal volatility regime** post-halt. They are not representative of the structural end-of-day inventory-unwind hypothesis.

**Nova recommendation:** flag per-session `circuit_breaker_fired: bool` in the Phase F session feature row. Riven post-mortem 3-bucket framework consumes this flag — sessions with circuit-breaker firings are candidates for the `inconclusive` bucket if they materially skew the sample, OR are kept in `strategy_edge` if the strategy genuinely tolerates the regime. The flag enables the diagnostic; the bucket assignment is Riven's call per ESC-011 R9.

Detection signal in our parquet: a continuous `aggressor=NONE` gap exceeding 30 minutes during a normal continuous-trading window (09:30 — 17:55 BRT) is the empirical signature. Pure timestamp gap analysis on the trade-tape suffices; no explicit `halt_flag` column exists in the historic parquet.

## §5 Cross trades (`tradeType=1`)

### §5.1 Definition

Cross trades are negotiated trades between two counterparties served by the **same broker**, executed off the public matching engine but registered for clearing. They print as `tradeType=1` on the consolidated tape and **carry no directional aggressor signal**.

### §5.2 Phase F implication

- **NOT price discovery:** the price was negotiated bilaterally, not via continuous matching against the public book.
- **NOT directional:** neither side is "aggressor" in the order-flow sense; CVD, OFI, imbalance features that consume aggressor sign MUST exclude `tradeType=1`.
- **MAY contribute to volume** if the analysis specifically targets total notional traded (rare in our spec).

**Nova recommendation:** Phase F harness emits per-event flag `cross_trade: bool` (True iff `trade_type=1` in live; in historic parquet, this flag CANNOT be set because the discriminator is absent — see §1.3). Document this gap explicitly in the determinism stamp under `data_quality.cross_trade_flag_available: false` for Phase F runs.
